Group Reflection:

As in assignment 2, the areas that we have excelled in are our communication skills, teamwork and adaptability. We have really started to get to know each other now and continue to marvel at each other's kindness and eagerness to help each other.

We have really started to evolve as a professional team too. We have honed our meeting skills, organisational skills, are more familiar with the technology involved (GitHub, HTML, CSS, etc.), and we carry on with our meetings, regardless of if not all members can attend. We have learned a lot about working within a team and each took a turn as leader at one point or another. We have developed some good skills with academic writing and other professional skills such as project management, leadership and collaborative documentation.

The dynamic has been slightly different this time around, with group members facing increasing pressures from study, work and family commitments, we have had to be more adaptable than ever. The loss of one team member and erratic and busy schedules of others has meant that the workload has felt heavier at times and we have had to constantly reassign and redistribute the load amongst the members that are available at the time.

While we have continued to evolve and better ourselves as a group, there are still some key areas in which we could improve. If we were to get started at the earliest opportunity, it probably would have relieved a lot of pressure from us. While we did set up some meetings early in the piece, we probably would have benefited by prioritising setting a clear definition for the aim and the scope of the project right at the start. As it played out, the segments were completed in a more modular way and we were often changing already completed sections so that they would still fit the context created by newer sections as the project evolved.

At times, this project seemed beyond our grasp, as there was so much going on in our lives. Sickness, hospital visits, holidays, state lockdowns, increasing workloads, family drama, new jobs and engagements, are some of the things that slowed us down with this assignment and those of us that survived till the end are very surprised and proud of each other’s tenacity and dedication to the team.
